The sparx reader trick

Those pesky questions that need you to get all correct or die trying wont be much of a pain anymore!

What is sparx reader anyway?

If you haven't heard of sparx reader you should be thankful as, to many students, the website is just horrible, the questions are too difficult and make you restart even if you get one wrong. Additionally it also gives out points painfully slowly. Makes reading feel like a chore and not something that is done for pleasure. Essentially, Sparx reader is used by schools for english (specifically reading) homework. You are given an extract of a story which the reader chooses and after reading, will be given questions which in order to finish your homework, you must get most of them correct. But the main problem is when you don't get it first try; on your next try, you need to get everything correct or suffer. Fortunetly, i have a solution

So what's the trick?

Searching the webpage for clues. You see, after you fail your first attempt and try again, you see the story which, using a builtin feature in your web browser, can be exploited. Most browsers have something known as "find in page" ,"search" or "ctrl f" which can allow you to search stuff in the story much quicker. Using this, we can search key words in the question in the story w/o scanning the entire story. For example, your question could be After Sophie drank tea, what did she do after? so using the trick, search for Sophie and cycle through the mentions of it until you go past her drinking tea. Once you've found the answer, submit it. It's not very powerful, but it can speed stuff up.

Is there a catch?

No! You don't even need to install anything to get up and running. And, it's free. Yay!
